# VirtEngine Acceptable Use Policy

**Last Updated:** January 29, 2026  
**Effective Date:** January 29, 2026

## 1. Introduction

This Acceptable Use Policy ("AUP") defines prohibited activities and acceptable conduct when using the VirtEngine decentralized cloud computing marketplace, blockchain network, and associated services (the "Services").

This AUP is incorporated into and part of our [Terms of Service](TERMS_OF_SERVICE.md). Violations may result in suspension or termination of access, removal of content, and/or legal action.

## 2. General Principles

All users must:
- Comply with all applicable laws and regulations
- Respect the rights and safety of others
- Use the Services in good faith
- Not interfere with network operations or other users
- Report security vulnerabilities responsibly

## 3. Prohibited Activities

### 3.1 Illegal Activities

You must not use the Services to:
- Violate any local, state, national, or international law
- Engage in fraud, money laundering, or terrorist financing
- Traffic in illegal goods or services
- Violate export control or sanctions laws
- Infringe intellectual property rights (copyright, trademark, patent, trade secret)
- Facilitate illegal gambling or unregistered securities offerings
- Distribute child sexual abuse material (CSAM)
- Engage in human trafficking or exploitation

### 3.2 Identity and Verification Fraud

You must not:
- Submit false, misleading, or stolen identity information
- Use synthetic identities or deepfakes to bypass VEID verification
- Impersonate another person or entity
- Create multiple accounts to circumvent restrictions
- Sell, transfer, or share VEID verification credentials
- Use stolen or fraudulent identity documents
- Manipulate biometric verification systems (spoofing, replay attacks)
- Bypass liveness detection mechanisms

**Consequences:** Identity fraud will result in immediate account termination and referral to law enforcement.

### 3.3 Network Attacks and Abuse

You must not:
- Launch denial-of-service (DoS) or distributed denial-of-service (DDoS) attacks
- Conduct port scanning, network probing, or vulnerability scanning without authorization
- Attempt to gain unauthorized access to systems, networks, or accounts
- Deploy malware, viruses, trojans, ransomware, or other malicious code
- Interfere with blockchain consensus mechanisms
- Submit invalid or malicious transactions to disrupt the network
- Conduct 51% attacks or similar consensus attacks
- Exploit smart contract vulnerabilities for personal gain
- Front-run transactions or engage in MEV (miner extractable value) manipulation
- Spam the network with excessive transactions

### 3.4 Marketplace Manipulation

You must not:
- Manipulate marketplace prices or engage in wash trading
- Create fake orders or bids to deceive users
- Engage in pump-and-dump schemes affecting token prices
- Collude with other users to manipulate auctions
- Provide false resource specifications as a provider
- Under-deliver on lease commitments
- Artificially inflate or deflate usage metrics

### 3.5 Data Protection Violations

You must not:
- Access or process others' personal data without authorization
- Scrape, harvest, or collect user data from the Services
- Re-identify anonymized or pseudonymous data
- Violate data protection laws (GDPR, CCPA, BIPA, etc.)
- Share or sell biometric data
- Process special category data without proper legal basis
- Violate consent restrictions in VEID data sharing

### 3.6 Abuse of Services

#### 3.6.1 Tenant Prohibited Uses

As a Tenant, you must not deploy workloads that:
- **Cryptocurrency Mining:** Unauthorized mining of cryptocurrencies (unless provider explicitly allows)
- **Spamming:** Send unsolicited bulk emails (spam) or messages
- **Phishing:** Conduct phishing campaigns or credential theft
- **Proxy/VPN Abuse:** Provide open proxies or VPN services for illegal activity
- **Content Distribution:** Distribute pirated software, media, or copyrighted content
- **Malicious Scanning:** Scan external networks for vulnerabilities
- **Botnets:** Operate command and control (C2) infrastructure
- **Abuse Relays:** Relay abusive traffic through leased resources

#### 3.6.2 Provider Prohibited Behaviors

As a Provider, you must not:
- Access tenant data without explicit permission (except as required by law)
- Tamper with tenant workloads or deployments
- Provide resources below committed specifications
- Falsify usage metrics or overcharge tenants
- Discriminate against tenants based on protected characteristics
- Use tenant data for competitive purposes
- Fail to implement adequate security isolation between tenants

#### 3.6.3 Validator Prohibited Behaviors

As a Validator, you must not:
- Censor transactions selectively (except for clearly illegal content)
- Collude with other validators to manipulate consensus
- Fail to maintain uptime commitments
- Double-sign blocks intentionally
- Submit false attestations
- Fail to properly secure validator keys

### 3.7 Prohibited Content

You must not upload, deploy, store, or transmit:
- Child sexual abuse material (CSAM)
- Content promoting terrorism or violent extremism
- Content inciting violence, hatred, or discrimination
- Revenge pornography or non-consensual intimate images
- Graphic violence or torture content (except in educational/documentary contexts with appropriate warnings)
- Malware, exploits, or hacking tools (except for security research with proper disclosure)
- Stolen credentials, credit card numbers, or personal data
- Counterfeit goods or fraudulent documents

### 3.8 System Integrity and Security

You must not:
- Reverse engineer, decompile, or disassemble the Services (except as permitted by open source licenses)
- Modify or create derivative works of proprietary components
- Remove or obscure proprietary notices, labels, or marks
- Circumvent access controls, authentication, or security measures
- Use automated tools to access the Services without authorization
- Overload or stress-test systems without permission
- Exploit vulnerabilities without responsible disclosure
- Access non-public APIs or systems

### 3.9 Governance Manipulation

You must not:
- Purchase or sell voting rights in governance proposals
- Bribe validators or delegators for votes
- Submit fraudulent or spam governance proposals
- Use multiple identities to bypass governance restrictions
- Engage in Sybil attacks on governance mechanisms

## 4. Resource Usage Limits

### 4.1 Fair Use Principles

**Tenants** must:
- Use leased resources within contracted limits
- Not "burst" beyond committed capacity without provider approval
- Terminate leases when resources are no longer needed
- Monitor and optimize resource consumption

**Providers** must:
- Allocate resources fairly among tenants
- Not oversell capacity beyond sustainable limits
- Implement proper resource isolation and QoS

### 4.2 Abuse of Free Tier or Trials

If we offer free tiers or trial periods:
- Do not create multiple accounts to abuse free resources
- Do not use free tiers for production workloads
- Do not transfer free resources to other accounts
- Comply with time and resource limits

## 5. Intellectual Property Respect

### 5.1 Copyright Compliance

Respect copyright laws:
- Do not infringe others' copyrights
- Comply with DMCA (Digital Millennium Copyright Act) and similar laws
- Honor copyright notices and licenses
- Obtain proper licenses for software and content

### 5.2 Trademark Respect

- Do not infringe trademarks or create confusion about affiliation
- Do not use VirtEngine trademarks without permission
- Honor provider and tenant brand guidelines

### 5.3 Open Source Compliance

When using open source software:
- Comply with open source licenses (Apache, MIT, GPL, etc.)
- Honor attribution and notice requirements
- Do not violate copyleft provisions
- Respect license restrictions

## 6. Compliance with Regulations

### 6.1 Financial Regulations

If using the Services for financial activities:
- Comply with KYC/AML regulations
- Honor sanctions and embargo lists
- Follow money transmission laws
- Respect securities regulations

### 6.2 Data Protection Laws

Comply with applicable data protection regulations:
- GDPR (EU)
- CCPA/CPRA (California)
- BIPA (Illinois)
- PIPEDA (Canada)
- LGPD (Brazil)
- PDPA (Singapore)
- Other jurisdictional requirements

### 6.3 Export Controls

- Do not export controlled technologies to restricted countries
- Comply with U.S. export control laws (ITAR, EAR)
- Honor international export restrictions
- Respect technology transfer regulations

### 6.4 Industry-Specific Regulations

If operating in regulated industries:
- Healthcare: Comply with HIPAA, HITECH
- Finance: Comply with PCI DSS, financial regulations
- Government: Comply with FedRAMP, ITAR, other requirements
- Other sectors: Follow applicable industry standards

## 7. Responsible Disclosure

### 7.1 Security Vulnerability Reporting

If you discover a security vulnerability:
- **Report Responsibly:** Email security@virtengine.com with details
- **Do Not Exploit:** Do not exploit the vulnerability or access unauthorized data
- **Do Not Disclose Publicly:** Allow reasonable time for remediation before public disclosure
- **Provide Details:** Include reproduction steps, impact assessment, and suggested fixes

### 7.2 Bug Bounty Program

We may offer a bug bounty program. Participants must:
- Follow responsible disclosure guidelines
- Not cause harm or access user data
- Comply with program terms
- Work collaboratively with our security team

## 8. Cooperation with Law Enforcement

### 8.1 Legal Obligations

We cooperate with law enforcement agencies and comply with valid legal process:
- Subpoenas and court orders
- Search warrants
- National security requests (with appropriate legal review)

### 8.2 User Cooperation

You must:
- Respond to legitimate law enforcement requests
- Not use the Services to obstruct justice
- Not tamper with evidence subject to legal holds

### 8.3 Emergency Disclosure

We may disclose information without legal process in emergencies involving:
- Imminent threat to life or safety
- Child exploitation
- Terrorist activity
- Other exigent circumstances

## 9. Monitoring and Enforcement

### 9.1 Monitoring Rights

We reserve the right to:
- Monitor network traffic for security and abuse
- Review content for compliance with this AUP
- Investigate suspected violations
- Analyze usage patterns to detect abuse
- Use automated tools for fraud detection

**Limitations:** We do not routinely monitor private communications or encrypted data.

### 9.2 Enforcement Actions

Violations may result in:

**Warnings:** First-time or minor violations  
**Content Removal:** Deletion of prohibited content  
**Suspension:** Temporary account suspension  
**Termination:** Permanent account termination  
**Slashing:** Forfeiture of staked tokens (validators/providers)  
**Legal Action:** Civil or criminal prosecution  
**Reporting:** Referral to law enforcement or regulatory authorities

### 9.3 Appeals Process

If your account is suspended or terminated:
- You may appeal by emailing appeals@virtengine.com
- Provide evidence supporting your position
- We will review appeals within 30 days
- Decisions after appeal are final

**No Appeal:** Identity fraud, CSAM, and severe violations are not eligible for appeal.

## 10. Provider and Tenant Responsibilities

### 10.1 Provider Responsibilities

Providers must:
- Implement proper security isolation (namespaces, containers, VMs)
- Not access tenant data without consent (except legal obligations)
- Honor lease terms and SLAs
- Report usage metrics accurately
- Maintain infrastructure security and updates
- Provide emergency contact information
- Respond to abuse complaints promptly

### 10.2 Tenant Responsibilities

Tenants must:
- Use resources for lawful purposes only
- Respect provider acceptable use policies (if more restrictive)
- Not exceed resource allocations
- Respond to abuse complaints
- Maintain security of deployed applications
- Back up important data
- Remove data before lease termination

## 11. Third-Party Services

If using third-party services integrated with VirtEngine:
- Comply with third-party acceptable use policies
- Respect third-party terms of service
- We are not responsible for third-party violations

## 12. Reporting Violations

### 12.1 How to Report

Report AUP violations:
- **Email:** abuse@virtengine.com
- **Subject Line:** Include "AUP Violation Report"
- **Details:** Provide evidence, user addresses, timestamps, and description

### 12.2 What to Include

- Description of the violation
- Evidence (screenshots, logs, transaction hashes)
- Your contact information (for follow-up)
- Affected user addresses or identifiers
- Urgency level

### 12.3 Response Time

- **Critical violations** (CSAM, terrorism): Immediate response
- **High severity** (network attacks, fraud): Within 24 hours
- **Medium severity** (spam, minor abuse): Within 72 hours
- **Low severity** (policy clarifications): Within 7 days

### 12.4 Confidentiality

We treat abuse reports confidentially but may:
- Share with law enforcement as required
- Disclose to affected parties
- Publish anonymized statistics

## 13. Amendments

We may update this AUP at any time. Changes are effective upon posting with an updated "Last Updated" date.

**Material Changes:** We will notify users of material changes via email or in-app notification.

**Continued Use:** Continued use of the Services after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated AUP.
