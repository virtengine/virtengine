# Control Objectives

The objectives below align to SOC 2 common criteria and the Availability and Confidentiality criteria. They define the outcomes that controls must achieve.

## Security (Common Criteria)

- CC1 Governance: Maintain documented security policies, ethical standards, and oversight responsibilities.
- CC2 Communication: Communicate security expectations internally and to external stakeholders.
- CC3 Risk Assessment: Identify, analyze, and prioritize risks to systems and data.
- CC4 Monitoring: Monitor controls, remediation status, and risk posture over time.
- CC5 Control Activities: Implement controls for access, changes, and operations.
- CC6 Logical Access: Enforce least privilege, strong authentication, and session management.
- CC7 System Operations: Detect, respond to, and recover from security events.
- CC8 Change Management: Control and document system changes and deployments.
- CC9 Risk Mitigation: Manage vendor and third party risks.

## Availability

- A1 Capacity and Performance: Monitor capacity, establish thresholds, and scale safely.
- A1 Continuity: Maintain backups, disaster recovery procedures, and recovery testing.

## Confidentiality

- C1 Data Protection: Classify data, encrypt data in transit and at rest, and limit access based on need.
- C1 Retention and Disposal: Retain and dispose of data according to policy and contractual commitments.
